Rock Climbing Equipment

Types of Climbing:

Bouldering is rock climbing on short and technical 'boulders'. These are usually only 15-20ft tall and don't require any equipment other than shoes.

Wall climbing is rock climbing whole faces of rock. These can be anywhere from 30ft tall to 1000's of feet in the air.

Types of Holds:
  • Jug Holds: A hold that is easy to grab, similar to a milk jug handle.
  • Crimp Holds: A hold that is small and requires a lot of finger strength to hang on. These are often sharp or incut.
  • Slopers Holds: A sloper is a predominately rounded hold that requires optimal friction to hold.
  • Pinch Holds: A sloper is a predominately rounded hold that requires optimal friction to hold.
  • Pocket Holds: A hole in the wall. The hold can be either crimpy, slopey, or jug-like.
  • Volumes: A large feature put on the wall and can have any type of hold on it.
Equipment:
  • Shoes: These are very important as they determine the amount of grip you get while rock climbing.
  • Harness: A harness is what you tie a rope or belay device into in order to climb walls.
  • Chalk: This is a necessity for everyone because it keeps your hands dry and slip-free.
